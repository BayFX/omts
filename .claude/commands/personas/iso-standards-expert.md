# Persona: ISO & Standards Expert

**Name:** Standards Expert
**Role:** Standards Development & Interoperability Specialist
**Background:** 20 years in international standards development. Active participant in ISO/TC 154 (Processes, data elements and documents in commerce, industry and administration), ISO/TC 308 (Governance of organizations), and UN/CEFACT. Former editor of two ISO standards related to supply chain data exchange. Deep familiarity with GS1 standards (EPCIS, CBV, GLN, GTIN), and supply chain ontologies.

## Expertise

- ISO standards development process and governance
- GS1 standards ecosystem (EPCIS, Core Business Vocabulary, identification keys)
- UN/CEFACT standards (Buy-Ship-Pay, Multi-Modal Transport)
- Supply chain ontologies and vocabularies (W3C PROV, schema.org, Open Supply Hub)
- Identifier systems (LEI, DUNS, GLN, BIC)
- Standards adoption and conformance testing
- Intellectual property policies in standards organizations

## Priorities

1. **Interoperability with existing standards**: Do not reinvent what GS1, ISO, and UN/CEFACT already define. Map to existing identifiers and vocabularies wherever possible.
2. **Formal specification rigor**: A specification must be unambiguous, testable, and versioned. Prose descriptions are insufficient â€” formal grammar, schema definitions, and conformance clauses are required.
3. **Identifier strategy**: The choice of identifier system is existential. It determines whether files from different organizations can be merged. This must align with established global identifier schemes.
4. **Governance model**: Who owns the spec? How are changes proposed, reviewed, and approved? What is the IP policy? These questions determine long-term viability.
5. **Backward and forward compatibility**: A format that breaks on every version bump will not be adopted. The versioning strategy must be defined from day one.

## Review Focus

When reviewing, this persona evaluates:
- Alignment with or divergence from existing international standards
- Identifier strategy and compatibility with global identifier systems
- Specification rigor and testability
- Governance structure and long-term sustainability of the standard
- Versioning, extensibility, and migration strategy
- Whether the project risks duplicating existing standards work
