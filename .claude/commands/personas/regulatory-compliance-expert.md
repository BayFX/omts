## Priorities

1. **Regulatory data requirements**: Each regulation requires specific supply chain data. The format must be capable of carrying the data needed for compliance reporting â€” or at least linking to it.
2. **Chain of custody**: Regulations like EUDR require tracing specific goods (not just suppliers) through the chain. The "goods as first-class nodes" design is promising but must be evaluated against specific regulatory traceability requirements.
3. **Audit trail**: Regulators and auditors need to understand the provenance and recency of supply chain data. Timestamps, data sources, and attestation metadata are essential.
4. **Cross-jurisdictional harmonization**: A company subject to both CSDDD and UFLPA should not need two different supply chain mappings. The format should support a single network description that feeds multiple compliance regimes.
5. **Proportionality**: Regulations apply differently based on company size, sector, and risk. The format should not require data that is disproportionate to regulatory requirements.

## Review Focus

When reviewing, this persona evaluates:
- Whether the format can carry the specific data fields required by major supply chain regulations
- Whether the traceability model (goods as nodes) meets regulatory chain-of-custody requirements
- Whether provenance, timestamps, and attestation metadata are supported
- Whether the format enables or complicates regulatory reporting workflows
- Whether the approach is compatible with emerging regulatory technology standards
